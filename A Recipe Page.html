Mutton Karahi

Mutton Karahi is a popular South Asian dish made with tender pieces of mutton cooked in a rich and spicy tomato-based gravy. It is traditionally prepared in a karahi, which is a type of wok used in South Asian cooking. The dish is known for its bold flavors, aromatic spices, and vibrant colors. It is often garnished with fresh coriander and ginger juliennes and served with naan or rice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg mutton, cut into pieces
  • 4 large tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 large onions, finely sliced
  • 4-5 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Julienned ginger (for garnish)
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a karahi or deep pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  5. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add the mutton pieces and cook on high heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the karahi, and let the mutton cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours or until tender. Stir occasionally and add water if needed to prevent sticking.
  8. Once the mutton is cooked, increase the heat to medium-high and cook uncovered for another 10-15 minutes to thicken the gravy.
  9. Sprinkle garam masala and mix well. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and julienned ginger before serving.

Serving Suggestions:

Serve Mutton Karahi hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice. It pairs well with a side of yogurt or raita to balance the spiciness of the dish.

Enjoy your delicious Mutton Karahi!
